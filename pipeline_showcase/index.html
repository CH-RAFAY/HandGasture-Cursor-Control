Control your computer with hand gestures.

A webcam-powered virtual mouse built with Python, OpenCV, MediaPipe, and PyAutoGUI for real-time cursor movement, click gestures, and smooth shutdown.

How it works

Vision detects the hand, then Python turns intent into mouse events.

Each webcam frame is analyzed by MediaPipe Hands, which returns landmark points for the thumb, index finger, middle finger, and palm. The application measures those positions and distances, then converts them into movement, clicks, and scroll behavior.

Why it matters

Natural interaction without touching the mouse.

This project demonstrates a practical use of computer vision in human-computer interaction. It is useful as a learning project, a demo system, and a base for more advanced gesture interfaces.

Gesture Map

Move Cursor

Point with your index finger and the cursor follows with smoothing.

Left Click

Bring your thumb and index fingertip together for a left click.

Right Click

Bring your thumb and middle fingertip together for a right click.

Scroll

Keep index and middle fingertips close and move up or down.

Reliability

Camera shutdown is intentional and complete.

Press Q, press Esc, or close the preview window once. The program releases the camera, closes the hand tracker, and destroys all OpenCV windows so the webcam is not left hanging in the background.
